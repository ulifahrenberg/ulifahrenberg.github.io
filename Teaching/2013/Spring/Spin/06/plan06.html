Theme of today's lectures: Fun with Spin

  1. A farmer wants to cross a river in a little boat, together with his dog, his goat, and a cabbage. The boat can only hold the farmer and one other thing (and the farmer does the rowing, so she needs to be in the boat for crossing the river). Also, if left alone, the dog will scare away the goat, and the goat will eat the cabbage.
    How can the farmer get all her things across the river, without the goat running away and without the cabbage being eaten?
    Write a Promela model of the system and use Spin to find a solution (if it exists).
  2. Have a look at the Frog Puzzle at http://www.hellam.net/maths2000/frogs.html. Write a Promela model of the puzzle (which works for an arbitrary number of frogs) and use Spin to solve it. How many frog moves does the solution take?
  3. How many frogs can your Promela model handle? Can you optimize it to handle more?
  4. At http://www.puzzles.com/products/RushHour/RushHourVideo.htm you will find the puzzle game Rush Hour. Model the game in Promela and use Spin to solve a few of the puzzles at http://www.puzzles.com/products/RushHour/RHfromMarkRiedel/Jam.html.
  5. Can you find solutions for Rush Hour puzzles which take as few steps as possible? What if the cars can move independently (in parallel): can you find fastest solutions?

These are also the last two TP for this course, so if you have any questions, any old exercises you need help with, anything: this is the last chance you have for asking.
